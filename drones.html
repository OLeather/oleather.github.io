Story:

Due to the COVID-19 Pandemic, FIRST Robotics Team 1351 was forced to operate at home through online meetings for a majority of the 2021 season. As a result, we wanted to come up with some fun projects that the students could do at home. Myself and a few other team leaders put together an unofficial drone challenge in our team, where small groups of 3-4 students would design, build, and program their own drones. They were able to individually take parts and work on it from home, and each week we transferred supplies from house to house while making sure to be COVID-safe. Drones are very different than the usual robots that the team builds, so it was a fun challenge for the students while also being small enough to work on from home.


Myself and the other team leaders created criteria for the drone challenge, provided an education curriculum about drone design to the students, and mentored the small teams that participated in the challenge. I helped with planning the electronics and control systems of the drone. We ended up putting together 4 teams of students who were interested, and even got one drone to fly by the end of the 2021 season! We plan to continue the drones at the beginning of the 2022 season as a side project to hopefully get more off the ground.


The structure of the drones were 100% 3D printed, and consisted of a raspberry pi for the control board, four quadcopter motors, and various altitude, gyroscope, and accelerometer sensors. They were all designed, manufactured, and assembled from scratch by students on the team.
